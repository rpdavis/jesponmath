**Digital and paper assessments measure DIFFERENT things**:
- **Digital**: Measures **problem-specific proficiency** (which facts are known/unknown)

- No devices needed
- No technology issues
- Works anywhere (even outdoor classroom)

### Limitations:

**1. No Problem-Specific Timing** âš ï¸ **CRITICAL LIMITATION**

**The "Hidden Struggle" Scenario**:
```
Paper Assessment Result: 12/30 correct in 60 seconds
CPM: 12 (low)

What the paper shows:
- 12 correct
- 18 incorrect or blank
- Total CPM: 12

What the teacher CANNOT see:
- Did student spend equal time on all 30 problems?
- Or did they get stuck on just 1-2 problems?
```

**Scenario A**: Truly Struggling (Paper is accurate)
```
Time breakdown:
- 2s per problem across all 30 problems
- Knows ~12 facts, doesn't know 18
- CPM: 12 is accurate representation of fluency

Digital would show:
- 12 problems: "proficient" (quick, automatic)
- 18 problems: "doesNotKnow" (slow or wrong)
- Proficiency: 40% (12/30)
```

**Scenario B**: Stuck on One Problem (Paper is misleading)
```
Time breakdown:
- 1s each for 29 problems (knows them all!)
- 50s stuck on one problem (9+7) trying to figure it out
- Only completed first 12 before time ran out
- CPM: 12 (but actually fluent in 29!)

Digital would show:
- 29 problems: "proficient" (quick, automatic)
- 1 problem (9+7): "doesNotKnow" (very slow)
- Proficiency: 97% (29/30)
- Would auto-advance to next level

Paper shows:
- CPM: 12 (appears to be struggling)
- Would NOT advance
```

**Classroom Reality**:
- Teacher managing 20-25 students simultaneously
- **Cannot observe** individual student patterns
- **Cannot see** that Student B spent 50s on one problem
- **Cannot distinguish** Scenario A from Scenario B
- Paper data alone is **insufficient for precise diagnosis**

**Why This Matters for RSP**:
- RSP students often have processing speed differences
- Some get "stuck" on specific problems (perseveration)
- Some have test anxiety (freeze on hard problems)
- **Paper CPM conflates** fluency with test-taking behavior
- **Digital timing data** isolates the actual weak facts

**2. No Proficiency Levels**
- Just correct/incorrect
- Can't distinguish:
  - "Doesn't know" (never seen)
  - "Emerging" (learned recently, unstable)
  - "Approaching" (knows but slow)
  - "Proficient" (automatic)

**3. Snapshot, Not Trend**
- Shows performance on one day
- Doesn't track progress over time
- Can't see if improving or regressing

**4. Manual Scoring**
- Teacher must score 25+ papers
- 15-20 minutes per class
- Data entry required
- Potential for entry errors
